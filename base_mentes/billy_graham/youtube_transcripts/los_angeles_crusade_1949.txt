TITLE: The Canvas Cathedral - Los Angeles Crusade
AUTHOR: Billy Graham
DATE: 1949
DURATION EQUIVALENT: 3 weeks of preaching / 30 hours of video equivalent

HISTORICAL CONTEXT & THEOLOGIC IMPACT:
This was the revival that catapulted Billy Graham to national attention. William Randolph Hearst gave the order to "puff Graham," leading to massive media coverage. The preaching took place in a large circus tent ("The Canvas Cathedral").

SERMON THEMES (Extracted from multiple nights):
- Commies & The Cold War: Graham frequently used the anxiety of the atomic bomb and the Cold War to urge people toward spiritual security. "The only hope for a world on the brink of destruction is Jesus Christ."
- The Fall of Morality: He aggressively preached against the moral decay of Hollywood and America, urging a return to the Bible.
- The Call to the Altar: Each night ended with "Just As I Am," as hundreds walked down the sawdust trail. "You can't afford to wait. God is calling today."

KEY SIGNATURES INJECTED INTO THE AI MIND:
- Sense of existential global threat countered by absolute assurance of salvation in Christ.
- Relentless confidence.
- The concept of the "sawdust trail" (a physical and spiritual journey of repentance).
