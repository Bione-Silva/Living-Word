TITLE: The Value of a Soul
AUTHOR: Billy Graham
DATE: Preached multiple times (e.g., New York Crusade 1957)
DURATION EQUIVALENT: 45 minutes / 8,000 words transcript

SERMON SUMMARY & EXCERPTS:
Core Subject: Mark 8:36 "For what shall it profit a man, if he shall gain the whole world, and lose his own soul?"

"You have a body, but inside that body is a spirit, a soul. It is the part of you that lives forever. Long after your physical body has turned to dust, your soul will live on. 

Why is the soul so valuable?
1. Because of its eternal nature. You were made for eternity. 
2. Because of the price God paid for it. God didn't buy your soul with silver and gold, He bought it with the precious blood of His Son, Jesus Christ on the cross.
3. Because of what happens if it is lost. To lose your soul is to be separated from God forever. 

If you were to gain the whole world—every bank account, every piece of real estate, every crown—and lose your soul, you have made a terrible bargain. 

I am asking you tonight to make a choice. The Bible says 'Prepare to meet thy God.' The Bible says 'Now is the accepted time.' Don't wait. You may not have tomorrow. Come to the cross tonight. Repent of your sins. Say 'Lord Jesus, I need you.' And He will save your soul."

HOMILETICAL STRUCTURE:
- Direct opening stating the reality of death and eternity.
- Use of rhetorical questions ("What would it profit?").
- The climax always points to the cross and the shedding of blood as the transaction for the soul.
- Concludes invariably with the Altar Call.
